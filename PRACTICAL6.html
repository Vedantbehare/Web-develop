Stephen Hawking British physicist

British theoretical physicist and cosmologist

Stephen Hawking was a British theoretical physicist and cosmologist.
He was born on January 8, 1942 in Oxford, England.
Hawking was widely considered to be one of the greatest scientists of his time.
He was the first scientist to devise a cosmology that married the general theory of relativity and quantum mechanics,
and he made huge contributions to our understanding of black holes.

biographys :

  • Birth and Education: Born on January 8, 1942, in Oxford, England. Studied physics at University College, Oxford, and completed his PhD at Cambridge, specializing in cosmology and general relativity.
  • ALS Diagnosis: Diagnosed with amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS) at age 21, a motor neuron disease that eventually left him paralyzed.
  • Contributions: Known for his work on black holes, including the theoretical prediction of Hawking radiation, suggesting black holes emit radiation.
  • Popular Science Author: Wrote several books, most notably "A Brief History of Time", making complex scientific ideas accessible to the public.
  • Advocacy for Quantum Mechanics: Strong supporter of the many-worlds interpretation of quantum mechanics.
  • Legacy: Passed away on March 14, 2018, leaving a profound impact on science and inspiring millions around the globe.
